# Procurement Guidelines · Vendor Contracting

> **Policy ID:** `POL-PROC-2026.07`
> **Owner:** Procurement Center of Excellence
> **Version:** 2026.07
> **Applies to:** Any engagement of a third-party vendor for goods or services.

---

## 1. Purpose

Ensure vendor engagements follow a consistent process that manages spend, risk, and delivery quality.

## 2. Approval thresholds

| Contract value (USD/year) | Required approvers |
| --- | --- |
| &lt; 25,000 | Requester + Line Manager |
| 25,000 – 250,000 | + Procurement Lead |
| 250,000 – 1,000,000 | + Category Owner + Legal |
| &gt; 1,000,000 | + CFO delegate |

Contracts touching regulated data require Compliance sign-off regardless of value.

## 3. Payment terms

- **Standard:** Net-45 from receipt of a valid invoice.
- Payment terms shorter than **net-30** require Finance approval.
- Prepayment (advance) is discouraged and requires explicit Category Owner approval.

## 4. Preferred templates

- **MSA + SOW** is the preferred structure for ongoing services.
- **NDA (mutual)** is the default; one-way NDAs are only used when the enterprise is solely disclosing.
- **SOW-only** (no MSA) is only acceptable for one-off engagements under $25k.

## 5. Non-standard term escalation

Any request that deviates from the approved clause library — payment, liability, termination, indemnity — must be routed for approval per the [Legal Policy](legal_policy.md). Do NOT bypass this in the interest of speed.

## 6. Vendor onboarding requirements

Before executing any contract, the vendor must have:

- A completed vendor questionnaire.
- Proof of insurance meeting the MSA minimums.
- W-9 / equivalent tax form on file.
- Passed the sanctions screening (see `compliance_policy.md`).

## 7. Renewals

- Auto-renewal clauses longer than **12 months** are non-standard and require Legal approval.
- Procurement will flag any contract 90 days before its renewal window opens.

## 8. Vendor performance

Category Owners shall review vendor performance annually and record it in the vendor register. Poor-performing vendors may be placed on a watchlist that requires additional approval for new SOWs.
