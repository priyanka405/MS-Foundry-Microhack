## Standard clause (verbatim — do NOT alter)

> **Termination.**
> *(a) Termination for Convenience.* Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing the other Party with **ninety (90) days'** prior written notice.
> *(b) Termination for Cause.* Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (i) commits a material breach and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or (ii) becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, or has such a petition filed against it that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.
> *(c) Effect of Termination.* Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement: (i) Provider shall promptly deliver to Client all work-in-progress and Client Materials; (ii) Client shall pay Provider for Services actually performed and expenses actually incurred through the effective date of termination; and (iii) all provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination shall survive, including confidentiality, limitation of liability, indemnification, and payment obligations then due.

## Plain-English summary

- **For convenience:** either side, 90 days' notice, no fee.
- **For cause:** immediate, if the other side commits a material breach and doesn't fix it within 30 days, or becomes insolvent.
- **On exit:** Provider hands over work-in-progress; Client pays for actual work performed; confidentiality and liability terms survive.

## When to use

- Default in every MSA and SOW.

## When to escalate

- Requests to change the notice period (e.g., 30 days, 180 days, or to remove convenience termination entirely) are non-standard — escalate per the [Legal Policy](../policies/legal_policy.md) §4.
- Requests to remove survival of confidentiality or liability terms are non-standard.
