# Approved Clause — Termination

> **Clause ID:** `CLL-TERM-v1.4`
> **Applies to:** MSA, SOW, DPA
> **Owner:** Legal Ops
> **Last reviewed:** 2025-01-31

## Approved wording (verbatim)

> **Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for the Initial Term of **[Initial Term — default three (3) years]** unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section. Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one-year Renewal Terms unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least **ninety (90) days** before the end of the then-current term.
>
> **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any affected SOW) upon written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed over a substantial part of its assets; or (c) undergoes a **change of control** to a direct competitor of the terminating Party, subject to a **sixty (60) day** notice window from the change of control.
>
> **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any active SOW) for convenience on **sixty (60) days'** prior written notice. Customer shall pay for Services actually performed through the effective date of termination, plus non-cancellable third-party commitments reasonably incurred by Supplier prior to receipt of the notice.
>
> **Effect of Termination.** On termination or expiration: (i) all licenses granted by Supplier to Customer that were paid for in full survive on a perpetual basis for the version delivered; (ii) each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information subject to legal-hold obligations; (iii) all accrued but unpaid fees become due; (iv) provisions that by their nature should survive (confidentiality, IP, indemnity, liability, dispute resolution) survive.

## Rationale

- **90-day non-renewal notice** — long enough to run a replacement RFP.
- **30-day cure period** — market standard; anything shorter (e.g. "immediate") is a red flag.
- **Termination for convenience** with a **60-day** notice window balances flexibility and supplier planning.
- **Change-of-control termination** protects against being locked into a contract now owned by a competitor.

## Deviations that MUST be flagged

The CLM Agent flags any of the following:

- Termination language including "**without notice**" or "**immediate**" for a breach type other than insolvency or willful misconduct.
- Cure period shorter than **fifteen (15) days** (below-market and disputable).
- Removal of the **change-of-control** right.
- Auto-renewal with a non-renewal window **longer than 90 days** (traps Customer).
- Termination-for-convenience notice longer than **120 days** (excessive exit friction).

## When a deviation is acceptable

- Extended cure period (60–90 days) for large, complex outsourcing where a 30-day cure is not physically feasible.
- Shorter termination-for-convenience notice (30 days) for early-stage pilots.
