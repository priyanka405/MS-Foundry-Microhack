# Approved Clause — Limitation of Liability

> **Clause ID:** `CLL-LIAB-v2.0`
> **Applies to:** MSA, SOW, DPA (as reference)
> **Owner:** Legal Ops (General Counsel approval required for any deviation)
> **Last reviewed:** 2025-02-20

## Approved wording (verbatim)

> **Limitation of Liability.** Except for (a) breach of confidentiality obligations, (b) each Party's indemnification obligations, (c) either Party's gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud, and (d) either Party's payment obligations, each Party's total aggregate liability arising out of or relating to this Agreement, whether in contract, tort, or otherwise, shall not exceed **an amount equal to the fees paid or payable by Customer to Supplier under the applicable SOW during the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim**, and in no event less than **fifty thousand euro (€50,000)**.
>
> Neither Party shall be liable for **indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages**, or for **lost profits, lost revenue, or lost data**, arising out of or relating to this Agreement, even if advised of the possibility of such damages, except in the case of the carve-outs (a)–(d) above.

## Rationale

- **Cap = 12 months of fees, minimum €50k** — proportional to the deal size, non-trivial floor for small SOWs.
- **Consequential-damage carve-out** — market standard; without it, exposure is unbounded.
- **Carve-outs** for confidentiality, indemnity, gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, and payment obligations — the four things a court would not want capped anyway.

## Deviations that MUST be flagged

The CLM Agent flags any of the following in a draft or third-party document, and refuses to silently modify them:

- Cap set as an absolute number below **€50,000** (e.g. "€1,000").
- Removal of the consequential-damage carve-out (would make the counterparty liable for lost profits).
- Removal of any carve-out under (a)–(d) above.
- Uncapped liability language ("without limitation", "notwithstanding anything to the contrary", etc.).

## When a deviation is acceptable

Only with **General Counsel** written approval attached to the contract file. Common approved deviations:

- Cap raised to **24 months of fees** for high-value MSAs where Customer has meaningful risk exposure.
- Additional carve-out for data-breach costs when Supplier is a processor of personal data at scale.
