# Approved Clause — Indemnification

> **Clause ID:** `CLL-INDEM-v2.1`
> **Applies to:** MSA, SOW
> **Owner:** Legal Ops (General Counsel approval for deviations)
> **Last reviewed:** 2025-02-20

## Approved wording (verbatim)

> **Indemnification.** Each Party (the **"Indemnitor"**) shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party, its affiliates, and their respective directors, officers, employees, and agents (the **"Indemnitee"**) from and against any third-party claim, together with reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs finally awarded or agreed in settlement, arising out of or relating to:
>
> (a) any breach of confidentiality obligations by the Indemnitor;
> (b) any infringement or misappropriation of a third party's intellectual property rights by the Indemnitor's services, deliverables, or materials;
> (c) any bodily injury, death, or physical damage to tangible property caused by the Indemnitor's gross negligence or willful misconduct; and
> (d) any breach by the Indemnitor of applicable law resulting in a fine or penalty imposed on the Indemnitee.
>
> The Indemnitee shall (i) promptly notify the Indemnitor in writing of the claim, (ii) grant the Indemnitor sole control of the defense and settlement (provided that any settlement admitting fault or imposing non-monetary obligations on the Indemnitee requires the Indemnitee's prior written consent), and (iii) cooperate reasonably in the defense at the Indemnitor's expense.
>
> **IP infringement remedy.** If Supplier's services or deliverables become, or in Supplier's opinion are likely to become, the subject of an infringement claim, Supplier shall, at its option and expense: (1) procure the right for Customer to continue using them; (2) modify them to be non-infringing while preserving materially equivalent functionality; or (3) if (1) and (2) are not commercially reasonable, terminate the affected SOW and refund the fees paid for the affected deliverables prorated over a **three (3) year** amortization from acceptance.
>
> **Exclusions.** The Indemnitor has no obligation under this Section to the extent a claim arises from (x) modifications made by the Indemnitee not authorized by the Indemnitor, (y) combination of the deliverables with items not provided by the Indemnitor where the claim would not have arisen but for such combination, or (z) use of the deliverables after notice to stop.

## Rationale

- **Scoped to third-party claims** — internal disputes are handled by warranty and remedies clauses, not indemnity.
- **IP indemnity with a repair-or-replace-or-refund remedy** — this is the market-standard "SaaS indemnity ladder" and materially reduces exposure.
- **Standard defense-and-cooperation mechanics** — notice, control, cooperation.
- **Exclusions** for customer modifications and unauthorized use — otherwise Supplier is liable for the Customer's own tampering.

## Deviations that MUST be flagged

The CLM Agent flags any of the following:

- **Removal** of the indemnity clause entirely.
- **Removal of the IP indemnity** (indent (b) above).
- Indemnity extended to "all direct and indirect damages" without limitation.
- Indemnity extended to **consequential damages** — that must live in the liability clause, not here.
- Waiver of the exclusions (x)–(z).

## When a deviation is acceptable

- **Mutual IP indemnity** in low-risk NDAs (default template has no indemnity in the NDA; some counterparties insist).
- Broader indemnity in **regulated sectors** (healthcare, financial services) — requires General Counsel approval and matching insurance evidence (§ 13 of the MSA).
